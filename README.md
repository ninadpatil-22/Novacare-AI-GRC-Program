# NovaCare AI — AI Governance Risk Assessment Program

## Project Overview
A comprehensive AI Governance Risk Assessment program built for NovaCare AI, 
a fictional healthtech SaaS company processing Protected Health Information (PHI) 
and cardholder data. This project demonstrates end-to-end GRC analyst skills 
across risk assessment, control gap analysis, policy development, and remediation 
planning.

## Frameworks Applied
| Framework | Purpose |
|---|---|
| ISO 42001 | AI Management System — primary governance anchor |
| NIST AI RMF | AI risk identification and treatment |
| ISO 27001:2022 | Information security control mapping |
| GDPR | EU personal data protection obligations |
| CCPA | California consumer privacy obligations |
| SOC 2 | SaaS audit and trust service criteria |

## Key Findings
- 6 enterprise AI tools assessed across clinical, engineering, operations and sales
- 12 risks identified — 5 Critical, 4 High, 3 Medium
- 5 unmitigated control gaps identified requiring immediate remediation
- AI governance posture assessed as **Developing** — controls exist in isolated 
areas but lack consistency, documentation, and formal oversight
- 4 P1 immediate actions identified with direct HIPAA, GDPR, and CCPA exposure

## Scenario
NovaCare AI is a fictional healthtech SaaS startup based in Austin, TX processing 
PHI on behalf of healthcare providers and cardholder data through integrated 
payment systems. The company employs 320 FTE and serves hospital networks and 
clinics across North America.
